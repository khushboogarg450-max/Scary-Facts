Notorious Haunted Locations

Aokigahara Forest

Japan

Known as the "Sea of Trees", this dense forest at the base of Mount Fuji is notoriously known for the high number of tragic events that occur within its depths. The iron-rich soil is said to interfere with compasses, leaving many hopelessly lost.

Poveglia Island

Italy

A small island near Venice that was used as a plague quarantine station and later a mental asylum. The soil is rumored to be 50% human ash, and the island is strictly off-limits to visitors today due to its dark history.

Hoia Baciu Forest

Romania

Often referred to as the "Bermuda Triangle of Transylvania", this forest is famous for intense paranormal activity, unexplained disappearances, and physical harm reported by those who dare to enter its twisted trees.

The Deep Archives

1959

Dyatlov Pass Incident

Nine experienced hikers fled their tent in the Ural Mountains in the dead of night, in sub-zero temperatures, without proper clothing. Their bodies were found weeks later with inexplicable injuries, some missing eyes and tongues, and trace amounts of radiation on their clothes.

1977

The Wow! Signal

A strong narrowband radio signal received by the Big Ear radio telescope in Ohio. The signal bore the expected hallmarks of non-terrestrial and non-solar system origin. It lasted for the full 72-second window but has never been detected again.

1947

SS Ourang Medan

A ghost ship where the entire crew was found dead, their faces frozen in expressions of sheer terror, staring at the sun with outstretched arms. Before the ship could be fully investigated, it caught fire and exploded, taking its secrets to the bottom of the ocean.

Urban Legends

Slender Man

An unnaturally tall, faceless figure in a dark suit who is said to stalk, abduct, or traumatize people, particularly children. First appearing as an internet meme, the legend has taken on a terrifying life of its own.

Black-Eyed Children

Paranormal creatures that resemble children between the ages of 6 and 16, with pale skin and black eyes. They reportedly hitchhike or panhandle, inducing an overwhelming sense of dread in those they approach.

The Mothman

A winged humanoid creature with glowing red eyes. Seen in Point Pleasant, West Virginia, prior to the collapse of the Silver Bridge, it is often considered a harbinger of doom.

Kuchisake-onna

The "Slit-Mouthed Woman" from Japanese folklore. A malicious spirit carrying a pair of scissors who asks victims if they think she is beautiful before revealing her terrifyingly mutilated face.

Cryptid Sightings

Chupacabra

The Americas

Meaning "goat-sucker", this creature is blamed for the death of livestock by draining their blood. Descriptions vary from a reptilian, alien-like entity to a hairless, mangy canine.

Skinwalkers

Navajo Lore

A type of harmful witch who has the ability to turn into, possess, or disguise themselves as an animal. They represent the antithesis of Navajo cultural values and are deeply feared.

Wendigo

Algonquian Folklore

A mythological, flesh-eating monster or evil spirit native to the northern forests of Nova Scotia, the East Coast of Canada, and Great Lakes Region of Canada and the United States.
